Selling the Family Home in a Divorce — Made Simple

Dividing the family home is one of the hardest parts of a divorce. It's usually the largest shared asset, it's emotionally loaded, and coordinating a traditional sale — repairs, showings, negotiations — between two people who are separating can turn into an ongoing source of conflict. Our goal is to take that piece off your plate: a fast, fair, neutral sale that lets both of you divide the proceeds and move forward.

We're Evan and Alex — local, licensed buyers based in Sonoma County. We regularly work with divorcing homeowners, and often with both spouses and their attorneys, to keep the sale calm and even-handed.

Is the House Community Property?

California is a community property state, so a home purchased during the marriage is generally owned 50/50 — regardless of whose name is on the title. A home owned before the marriage, or received by gift or inheritance, is usually separate property, though that can get complicated. How your home is classified affects how the proceeds are split, so it's worth confirming with your attorney.

Your Options for the Home

  • One spouse buys out the other — if that spouse can refinance alone and afford the home going forward.
  • Sell and split the proceeds — the most common choice, giving both a clean financial break.
  • Deferred sale — co-owning for a set period (often until children finish school) before selling.

For a deeper walkthrough of each option, how community property works, and the legal guardrails once a divorce is filed, read our full guide: Selling a House During Divorce in California.

Divorce Home Sale FAQs — Sonoma & Marin County

Do we have to sell the house in a California divorce?

Not necessarily. You generally have three choices: one spouse buys out the other, you sell and split the proceeds, or you co-own for a set period. Selling is common when neither spouse can afford the home alone or both want a clean break, but it isn't required if you agree on another path.

Do both spouses have to agree to sell the home?

In most cases, yes. Once a divorce is filed in California, automatic restraining orders prevent either spouse from selling community property without the other's consent or a court order. We're used to working with both parties and their attorneys to keep the sale moving smoothly.

How do you keep the sale fair to both spouses?

We stay neutral. We provide one transparent cash offer, explain how we arrived at it, and treat both spouses and their attorneys the same. There's a single clear number to divide, which removes a lot of the friction that a traditional sale can create during a divorce.

How fast can you close?

As little as 7 days once the title is clear and both spouses have signed, or on whatever timeline fits your settlement. A quick, certain close lets both people divide the proceeds and move forward.

Do we have to make repairs or hold showings?

No. We buy as-is, so there are no repairs, no staging, and no showings to coordinate between two households. That removes a common source of conflict and delay during a divorce.
