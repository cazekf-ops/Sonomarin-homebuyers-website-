How to Sell an Inherited House in Sonoma County

By Evan & Alex, Sonomarin Home Buyers  |  Serving Sonoma & Marin County, CA

Inheriting a home in Sonoma County can feel overwhelming — especially when you're already dealing with the loss of a loved one. Between probate court, property taxes, maintenance costs, and family dynamics, the decisions can pile up fast. This guide walks you through everything you need to know about selling an inherited property in Sonoma County, including your options, the timeline, and how to make the process as smooth as possible.

```

Understanding Probate in California

```

Before you can sell an inherited home in Sonoma County, you need to understand where the property stands legally. If the deceased had a will, the estate typically goes through probate — a court-supervised process that validates the will and authorizes the transfer of assets. In California, probate can take anywhere from 9 months to 2+ years depending on the complexity of the estate. However, if the property was held in a living trust, you may be able to skip probate entirely and sell much faster. Your first step should be consulting with a probate attorney or the executor of the estate to understand exactly where things stand.

Your Options for Selling an Inherited Home

Once you have legal authority to sell, you have three main paths:

1. List on the MLS with a real estate agent. This typically gets you the highest price but requires repairs, staging, showings, and waiting 60-90+ days to close. If the home needs significant work — which many inherited properties do — this can mean spending tens of thousands upfront before seeing a dime.

2. Sell at auction. Auctions can move quickly but prices are unpredictable and fees can be significant.

3. Sell directly to a cash buyer. This is often the fastest and simplest option for inherited properties. A cash buyer will purchase the home as-is, handle the paperwork, and close on your timeline — often within 1-2 weeks. You don't need to clean out the house, make repairs, or deal with showings.

Common Challenges with Inherited Properties in Sonoma County

Inherited homes in Sonoma County come with a unique set of challenges:

Deferred maintenance. Many inherited homes haven't been updated in decades. Older wiring, aging roofs, and outdated plumbing can make traditional financing difficult for buyers — which is why cash sales are often the most practical solution.

Multiple heirs. When a property is inherited by several family members, everyone needs to agree on what to do. Disagreements about price, timing, or whether to sell at all can drag the process out significantly.

Out-of-area heirs. Many people who inherit Sonoma County property don't live here. Managing a property from a distance — dealing with maintenance, taxes, and potential tenants — is a significant burden that motivates many heirs to sell quickly.

Property taxes. Under Proposition 19 (passed in 2020), the property tax reassessment rules for inherited homes changed significantly in California. In most cases, inheriting a home now triggers a reassessment to current market value, which can mean substantially higher property taxes.

The Step-by-Step Process for Selling an Inherited Home

Step 1: Determine the legal status of the estate and your authority to sell.

Step 2: Get a property assessment — understand what repairs are needed and what the home is worth in its current condition.

Step 3: Decide on your selling strategy based on your timeline, the condition of the home, and your financial goals.

Step 4: If selling to a cash buyer, request offers from local buyers and compare. Make sure you understand exactly what each offer includes — some buyers deduct repair costs at closing.

Step 5: Open escrow with a local title company. They'll handle the legal transfer and make sure all liens and outstanding taxes are resolved at closing.

Step 6: Close and receive your funds — typically within 7-14 days with a cash buyer.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I have to go through probate to sell an inherited home in California?

Not always. If the property was held in a living trust, you can often sell without probate. If the estate's total value is under $184,500 (as of 2024), a simplified procedure may apply. For most inherited homes, however, probate is required and an attorney should be consulted.

```

Can I sell an inherited home before probate is complete?

In some cases yes — California allows a probate sale with court confirmation, which can happen while probate is still ongoing. A cash buyer experienced with probate sales can often navigate this process.

How long does it take to sell an inherited home in Sonoma County?

With a traditional listing, 3-6 months is common when you factor in probate, repairs, and time on market. With a direct cash sale, closing can happen in as little as 7-14 days once you have legal authority to sell.

What happens if multiple heirs disagree about selling?

All heirs with an ownership interest must agree to sell voluntarily. If agreement can't be reached, any heir can petition the court for a partition action — a legal process that forces the sale of the property and divides proceeds among heirs.
